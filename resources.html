Helpful Resources

This page offers a few introductory resources for those who would like to learn more about bhakti-yoga, chanting, kirtan, and devotional life. Whether you are completely new or already practicing, we hope these resources are helpful in your spiritual journey.

What is the Process of Chanting?

Chanting the Hare Krishna maha-mantra is a central practice of bhakti-yoga. It is a simple and powerful way to keep our minds concentrated on remembering the Supreme Personality of Godhead, Lord Krishna. It helps us purify our heart, increase concentration, and integrate spirituality within our daily lives.

Japa Meditation and Daily Practice

Japa is defined as the personal chanting of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra on beads. Many dedicated practitioners aim to chant a specified number of rounds each day as part of their sadhana, or spiritual practice.

What is Kirtan?

Kirtan represents the congregational chanting of the Hare Krishna maha-mantra, often accompanied by instruments such as the harmonium, kartals (hand cymbals), and mridanga (drum). It is both a joyful and deeply meditative experience which brings people together in spiritual sound.
